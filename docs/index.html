About This Course

This course provides a systematic treatment of international trade theory and policy, with particular emphasis on India’s agricultural trade. We begin with the classical foundations — Mercantilism, Absolute Advantage, and Comparative Advantage — and progress through modern trade theory (Heckscher–Ohlin, Stolper–Samuelson, Rybczynski), trade policy instruments (tariffs, quotas, subsidies, dumping), and open-economy macroeconomics (Balance of Payments, Foreign Exchange, Mundell–Fleming).

The second half of the course focuses on international institutions: the WTO, the Agreement on Agriculture, SPS/TBT measures, and India’s own export infrastructure (APEDA, MPEDA, DGFT). All lectures use recent Indian trade data and are illustrated with R/ggplot2 figures.

Course Design Notes

All lecture slides are built in Quarto RevealJS and include:

  • ggplot2 figures using live R code — all data embedded in slides (no external files required)
  • LaTeX mathematics — PPF/IC diagrams, welfare decompositions, Marshall–Lerner condition, Mundell–Fleming IS-LM-BP
  • India-first examples — every theoretical concept anchored to Indian agricultural trade data
  • Pedagogical approach inspired by MIT 14.54 (International Trade)
  • Notation follows Salvatore (2013): Nation 1/Nation 2, Commodity X/Y, welfare areas a, b, c, d
